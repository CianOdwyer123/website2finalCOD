In Dunboyne we have a few leisure spots. The main one of these would be the Dunboyne village public park.

This would be a great walk to bring your dog on so it can have a nice sniff. Also within this park there is a playground with many play areas for the younger kids. While the little kids are occupied in the playground, the older kids to teenagers can play at the skate park. The skatepark has 7 ramps and a bike rack.

Get The Train into Dublin City:

In Dunboyne we are blessed with a train station that will bring you anywhere in the country.


Get The Bus To Blanchardstown Shopping Centre or Dublin city:

Another form of transport is Dublin bus. This bus will bring you from Dunboyne all around Blanchardstown and Dublin City.

Community Centre:

In the community centre there is many services such as:


  • A gym
  • A Basketball court
  • A Badminton court
  • Tennis Court
  • Astro Turf 5 - a - side Pitches

GAA pitches:

Dunboyne is home to St Peters GAA club. This was originally a hurling club before football was also included in the 1940s.

St Peters has two pitches and games are run regularly. The club also runs a Kiddies club on a Sunday morning where all are welcome.

Although these pitches are used for matches and training, when they are not in use, the public can use them to puck a ball around. These pitches are specifically designed for the sports:

  • Hurling.
  • Gaelic football.

Soccer:

Dunboyne AFC is based in Dunboyne. The club has a range of soccer pitches and astro training areas.

The clubhouse was officially opened by Pele some years ago. All are welcome to come and cheer on Dunboyne.


  • Athletics:

    • Dunboyne Athletic club is the third largest in the country and has excellent facilities that include an all-weather 400m track complete with flood-lighting; changing rooms and hot showers; and a weights and gym area.
